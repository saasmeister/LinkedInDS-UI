One rule: the idea is the design; the styling gets out of its way. Every visual is built on a single mechanism — a shape, metaphor, fake interface, or chart — that makes one point land instantly. Colour is not part of the method: greys map onto the brand ramp, focal = the strongest tone.

1
One sentence

If you can't say the point in one line, it isn't ready.

2
Find the mechanism

Don't illustrate the sentence — encode it. "Almost there" → a bar stuck at 97%.

3
Steal a form

Terminal, receipt, funnel, gauge, subway map, tier list — bend a real object to a SaaS truth.

4
Thumbnail test

Shrink it. Concept still reads? Ship it.

65 archetypes (A1→I10) live in the library. Be bold, not tame — more literal on the metaphor, more minimal on the styling.
